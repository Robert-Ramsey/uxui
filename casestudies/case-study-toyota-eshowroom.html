Toyota Eshowroom Dealer Sales Tool

High Impact Selling Tool

Los Angeles based agency, Beyond Curious, brought me aboad to develop a UX strategy and create annotated wireframes for Toyota Eshowroom. The tool was currently in use but independent Toyota Dealers were unsure about the purpose of the Intranet tool and they find it difficult to locate information.

  • Get Dealer to visit site more frequently
  • Help Dealer Retrieve High Value Content Quickly, Easily,
  • Empower Dealers to Improve Their Own Sales Skills
TagsResourceProjectDate25-04-2017

Research

The highest sought information baed on search data was brought to the front and center location in a tabbed list. This eliminated searching completely for the highest value users searching the most relevant information. This significant location and presentation provides a clear meaning for the sites dealers that is supported by the overall improved ability to search and find the specific information they want and need.

Before and After Home Pages

The home page design contains a large hero unit that supports featuring top content and a large tab unit at the center stage location that aids in finding the most sought after content. The new global navigation is redesigned around product models and the dropdown chooser was redesigned to hold other features like notebooks and bookmards

Performance Support

To create a high impact selling tool the site would have to make finding things quick, encourage repeat visits and speak to users with a dealer-specific vocabulary that aided the sales process.

The design allows the dealer to stay in a subsection, for instance Features & Specs, while clicking through produt models on the main navigation. This design supported much easier repeated access as well as directly facilitated comparing vehicles.

The idea of providing "Performance Support" came out of earlier design workshops. To provide the best support for dealers on the job, the site would need a much quicker and more organic way to access, retrieve and retain information. Analytics showed the highest accessed content by Dealers was specific to company training and certification, product and technology facts, and customer and sales Information.

Feature Flows

Workflow diagrams helped the developers and other stakeholders visualize some of the required UI components and user interactions.

Content Modelling

I Also, created a product dashboard that allows navigation and comparison through large amounts of product data. Additionally, a content portal that builds on a new taxonomy and bubbles up the most relevant content and displays it in a clean, easily scannable and responsive format.

Search Before and After

To improve the search experience I moved the filters to a horizontal layout and provided a visual control allowing the user to show or hide the filter panel. Automaticall firing the search after each selectiona allows the user to iteratively build upon results as well as save nuanced search results .

Content Portal

 I worked with the content team to create a taxonomy that leveraged the current tagging scheme. The content portal provided a new readable format that supported independent browsing and learning.

Product Dashboard

Through content modelling, our, teams efforts led to a better grouping and display. Available data was reorganized and mapped to a product dashboard with 6 top level groupings. Vehicle Highlights, Options/Packages/Trims/Colors, Features & Specs, Competitive Advantage, Vehicle Accessories and Articles & Resources. A flyought menu was created to facilitated vertical scrolling through some of the longer pages.

Mobile Layouts

Responsive mobile views allow the tool to be used on the lot, on the showroom floor and offsite.
