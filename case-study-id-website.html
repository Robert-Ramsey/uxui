International DelighT website wireframes

Brand Refresh, Loyalty and Engagement

I was contracted by Direct Partners to build the main site for the non-dairy creamer brand International Delight for Whitewave Foods. They wanted a site that contributed to better feelings about the brand, Increased the purchase intent of web visitors and increase sales from promotional offers in their "Member Delights" members area..

TagsResourceProjectDate25-04-2017

Business Analysis

Whitewave provided good detail on the demographics for the site, however, on viewing the social media. It was clear the site was not meeting the needs of the users. May of the posts called for information about where to get products and directly called out the website for it’s poor performance in that area. The vast majority, however, were sharing a relatively robust conversation about their favorite flavors. The social visitors were having a conversation that the brand wasn’t taking part in

Competitive Landscape

Site Metrics

The analytics of the site showed that engagement in the logged in area was suffering. People needed a better reason to sign into the site. Since no resources were being spent to drive more traffic, it was important for the site to be visually engaging and support the robust conversation that was already taking place online.

Opportunity

The strategy deck layed out the vision and the creative execution for the client. Explaining the process and highlighting important strategic aspects of competitive sites, site traffic and user data.

I devised a four part solution that consisted of a better search results, a modern UI, a highly visual format and social integration. Some of the new feautures included:

  • A grid based modular, responsive layout
  • Improved site navigation
  • Magazine style layout
  • Rich search results page

Creative Deliverables

The wireframes incorporated a nice grid stucture and white space that provide an experience that was more alive and organic and supported the use and highighting of social content and good use of brand graphics and photography.
